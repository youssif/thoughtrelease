The First Week

Written Retroactively by Youssif Abdulhamid on July 10, 2013.

Each day offers a fresh beat of exhilaration. The feeling that literally every move I make -- every moment costing me time that could be otherwise invested in my family, friends, and fun -- is my own decision, independent of social pressures, brings its own unique high.

Having dropped out of the Hack Reactor application process in favor of App Academy, I now have officially one program I can be accepted into that fits with the leave of absence I have arranged with Google.

I am in the process of knocking out the 60 hours of App Academy prework. If I do well on that, I will be invited to take a timed coding exam (my second of the process) as the next step in the process. The exercises are challenging, but every conquest has its reward; small personal victories against The Machine. Programming, I’ve found, is a brilliantly rewarding experience. There exist moments of despair and frustration, confusion and self-doubt. But once that error message disappears; once that RSpec test passes; once the blinking cursor on the black terminal screen spits out green instead of red… programming brings its own unique high.

And yet, the clock ticks on.

The prework is taking me longer than I originally anticipated. Challenges you face while coding are not a matter of simply writing things out; once the wall is hit, it stays hit until the bulb lights and you identify your mental lapse against the machine. Sometimes, that requires throwing in the towel and calling a quits at 3:30 AM to look at it with a clear head the next morning. But I don’t have enough nights to spare towards my deadline. The application process is taking me longer than I expected; I expected to have been accepted into a program before I left Google. At best, I will know in about a week’s time. My arbitrary deadline, the “backing of my troops against the ocean”... it all feels so naive.

Every coding exercise begins to feel more like Revenge of the Nerds than it does a new learning experience.

The best I can do is run. I run furiously. I run at night, I run in the morning. I run up steep 50 degree inclines for miles at a time. I run until the problem racing through my head tires out and affords me some clear space in there.

And yet, the clock ticks on.

The pragmatist within me (the pesky mole likes to stay buried, but will sneak his head out the ground on occasion) prompts me to start hedging my bets a bit -- just in case this whole App Academy thing doesn’t quite work out. Browsing through Quora, I stumble upon a program listed called MakerSquare based in Austin. It’s the program with the nearest start date and completely overlaps with all the Ruby I have been learning. More importantly, my sister recently decided to move out there for a teaching job, and I have never been to Texas. I figure it could be an interesting summer adventure, in light of the growing realization that App Academy could very well not pan out for me. So I take a shot in the dark and apply.

And the clock ticks on.
